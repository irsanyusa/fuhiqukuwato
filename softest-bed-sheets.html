Discover my top picks and what I loved (and didn’t) about each sheet set.

What to consider when buying the softest bed sheets

There’s a lot to keep in mind when purchasing the softest bed sheets, including your own sleep style, desired material, budget, and preferred texture. Below, we break it all down.

Material and manufacturing techniques

When shopping for soft sheets, Julia Siemen, a certified sleep science coach at Sleep Advisor, tells us that material and manufacturing techniques are very important. “For warm sleepers, moisture-wicking fabrics like cotton and linen are very breathable, keeping you cool during the night,” she says. “For cold sleepers, flannel sheets may be a good option for warmth.”

From my own testing and speaking with friends and family, personal material preferences will undoubtedly differ. I love the instant softness bamboo sheets provide, while others might like a soft, crisp cotton sheet or perhaps the best percale sheets like you’d find at a luxury hotel.

Weave

Percale and sateen are two of the most common weave styles, with percale being more crisp, lightweight, airy, and breathable, as well as more prone to wrinkling. Sateen has a silky smooth finish and sheen, is a bit weightier/thicker, softer, and wrinkle-resistant, with an excellent drape. It’s also more prone to pilling and snags and retains heat more than percale.

Quality

Turns out, a high thread count doesn’t always make for softer sheets. While it matters, the quality of the fiber itself and the construction is more important. Look for long-staple or extra-long staple cottons for the softest sheets.

Certifications

Certifications are also important, according to Siemen. “The OEKO-TEX certification indicates environmentally and socially responsible production, GOTS makes sure they are organic, and the Eco-INSTITUT certification ensures products are free from pollutants and hazardous chemicals.”

Fit

Check the dimensions of sheets, the fitted sheet specifically, to ensure it’ll fit and cover your mattress.

Lifestyle preferences

“Considering personal preferences, lifestyle, and specific needs, such as sleeping temperature, budget, and desired texture, is important for choosing sheets for a good night’s sleep,” says Siemen.

Tips to help keep sheets soft

“Baking soda and vinegar, although different in composition, work well to dissolve residues and keep sheets soft,” says Siemen. “Baking soda is like a natural alternative to fabric softener: It breaks down organic compounds, softens sheets, and prevents detergent build-up, keeping your sheets breathable and helping them last longer.”

“Vinegar counteracts detergent or fabric softener residues because it's acidic,” she explains, adding that she advises against fabric softener if you want your sheets to stay soft, as its residue can make sheets stiff over time.

Other ideas to soften sheets without causing damage, according to Siemen, are to soak them in Epsom salts or borax overnight. You can also wash and repeat to get that super soft-to-the-touch, snuggly texture sooner. “If you wash your sheets multiple times, particularly for sheets like cotton or linen, it helps break them in over time, although it's smart to dry them on low heat or hang them dry to keep them soft,” she says.

How material type affects the softness of sheets

“The softness of sheets can be influenced by the type of material used in their construction,” notes Siemen. “Lower-quality sheets are often made from short-staple cotton fibers, resulting in a rougher texture. The material type and quality can also impact how the sheets retain their softness over time, making it important to consider the fabric when looking for long-lasting softness.”

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What is the softest type of bed sheets?

According to Siemen, the softest types of bed sheets are typically Egyptian cotton. “These sheets have a soft feel right from the initial use because of their superior quality and the longer fibers in their composition,” she says. “Linen sheets might feel stiff initially, but they tend to soften over time as they are used and washed, eventually becoming soft once they've been broken in.” Sheets made with premium, long-staple cottons and sateen weaves will also be luxuriously soft, as will bamboo viscose.

What are the softest sheets that don't pill?

Percale or sateen weaves, which, “are less prone to pilling due to their tight construction and smooth textures,” says Siemen. “Specifically, sateen weaves are an ideal choice, especially for those with a thread count not exceeding 700, as they provide a soft feel.”

What makes bed sheets super soft?

Bed sheets’ softness is based on factors including material, weave, how long you’ve owned/washed them, and thread count. “Sheets made from certain materials, like Egyptian or Supima cotton or bamboo, are soft right from the start,” says Siemen. “Sheets that have been washed and used over time also tend to become softer compared to brand-new ones straight out of the package.”

What thread count is best for soft sheets?

While the softness of sheets can correlate to thread count, Siemen says the ideal thread count will vary based on the fabric type. “For cotton sheets, a thread count of approximately 400 tends to be softer. For bamboo sheets, a thread count of around 300 is generally preferable for a softer feel. Linen sheets can be soft with a thread count of around 130.” For more info on the differences between the materials, check out our guide to bamboo vs. cotton sheets.

How often should you wash your sheets?

In terms of how often you should wash your sheets, most experts recommend changing or washing your sheets once a week to maintain maximum freshness. The pillows should stay protected by ensuring you also wash your pillowcases weekly.
